A few good non-BHO links:

Pohaku Ukuleles has the DEFINITIVE guide for installing PegHeds tuners. It features a step by step pictorial for Ukulele, but the basics apply to all PegHeds installations. I've been asked about how to install PegHeds on a banjo so many times it's just plain silly, so here it is.
Museum of Appalachia collections exhibit has a huge number of folk banjos as well as other instruments on display. (Dead link, but we'll hope it returns!)

The Banjo Sightings Database states that its mission is to collect, document, and centralize information about the construction and development of the early banjo and to act as a reference tool and clearinghouse for information about the early banjo.

The Minstrel Banjo Forum is dedicated to all aspects pertaining to the Minstrel Banjo. Construction, performance, and lots of assorted information is available there.

Mandolin

Mandolin Cafe Forum, if you have any questions you won't after a few visits.

Upright Bass

Bob Gollihur's Bass Website is easily the oldest, largest, most comprehensive source of all things Bass that you could possibly find. Plus Bob's a heck of a nice guy, to boot!

Bob Gollihur's Bass Links has SOOO much information and SOOO many links that it commands it's own link!.

The String Emporium is another popular bass-centric website dedicated to basses, bows and how to maintain them. Steve maintains a good selection of new and used uprights across a broad spectrum of price and also carries a full line of accessories to keep your bass happy and healthy. Steve can also help you out with your upright amplification needs, as he carries a full line of pickups, preamps, and amplifiers.

Rockabilly Bass is a site with lots of upright bass info and a active forum with many doghouse players.

Acoustic Guitar

The Acoustic Guitar Forum has probably the greatest collection of guitar-related geekiness on the web. Answer your questions about building, buying, playing, recording, and destroying acoustic guitars. (Well, maybe not the destroying part...)

Crossover Guitar details my personal foray into crossover guitar. If you don't know what it is you'll have to visit! I'll also be adding a bit of steel-strung acoustic guitar information from time to time, so keep your ears on!

Lap Steel and Pedal Steel Guitar

Brad’s Page Of Steel a place specifically devoted to the lap steel guitar aficionado!

Bob’s Steel Guitar Forum is your source for information related to any form of the steel guitar. Pedal, lap, Dobro and other players and builders hook up here.

Fiddle

Bowed Electricity is clearinghouse for info pertaining to electric violin.

The Fiddle and Alternative Strings Forum is a great place to look for the answers to all of your fiddle-related questions.

General Luthiery Information

Frets.com remains the internet's largest single source of construction and repair information. Frank Ford is truly a gift to mankind!

Luttao Mottola, luthier R.M Mattola’s “college of lutherie knowledge” features non-profit design, research and educational resources for the lutherie community.

Musical Instrument Maker's Forum is a wealth of information for the design and construction of musical instruments of all types. Join (it's free!) to search the library of archived discussions for the answer to any question you may have.

Rudy's Sketchbook of Musical Instrument Plans, Ideas, and Inspiration is my original website. Lots of instrument related-information there, too! Unfortunately it has limited space, bandwidth usage restrictions, and pop-up ads that forced me to migrate to this site, where I have total control over the content. Still, some useful stuff there if you can stomach the pop ups.

Recording Information

Rudy's Recording Page is my attempt to shed a bit of light on the recording process that's designed for newbies, but also details Mr. Preston Studios, my fairly-simple foray into home and location recording that's designed to spend more time playing and less time concentrating on expensive hardware and long learning curves.

RECORDING magazine has this excellent and detailed 28 part series detailing the complete process of recording that is essential reading. An excellent source of information and a great magazine to boot!

Sound On Sound is one of the few on-line magazines which makes a bunch of their past articles available for free. Kudos to them for an excellent source of information and a great magazine to boot!

The Basic Home Recording Studio website is one of the multitudes featuring "how-to" information for the beginning recordist. There's a substantial amount of good information here, with minimal emphasis placed on trying to get you to buy something. This site is certainly one of the better ones I've seen for newbies, and it takes you step by step through the process of setting up a basic home studio with lots of easy to understand explanations regarding core concepts of digital audio. Recommended by me, and that says a lot!

Reaper is my current favorite DAW. Free to try, inexpensive to license if you like it. This DAW is the closest thing to ProTools that you're going to get without the high cost. It's got a bit of a learning curve, but like my old friend Dave Mason once sang. "Something worth having doesn't come to easily. A man needs the challenge, or a man couldn't be." OK, now go challenge yourself.

Homebrewed Music is Fran Guidry's excellent site devoted to recording acoustic guitar, although you'll find lots of information about recording in general. Fran has an excellent "how-to" article he has written detailing how you can make your own gobo panels and has all the supporting evidence to prove how much they can singlehandedly improve your recordings.
