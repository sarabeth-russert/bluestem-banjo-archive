Bluestem Banjo Armrest Assembly Instructions

Armrest installed on a 24 hook rim:






What is supplied in "kit" form: 6-1/2" long by 3/4" wide armrest top plate, 4 support brackets (only 3 shown here...), 220 & 400 sandpaper, and 0000 steel wool.


JB Weld is used to bond the armrest plate to the metal supports.


Determine the desired mounting location. Ideally you'll be able to center the brackets so they look balanced. Closely spaced hooks may require 4 brackets for visual appearance; 4 are included in the kit. The armrest is shown being installed on a rim with 24 hook spacing. Cut a section of your favorite cereal box to match the outer diameter of the tension band and hold it in place with a bit of painter's masking tape.


Loosen the desired hooks and slip the brackets under and re-tighten them.


Mark the bend line for the brackets with a fine point marker. Number the brackets above the bend line so they can be removed and re-installed at the same locations.


Remove the brackets, grasp firmly at the bend line with slip joint pliers.


The bracket is easily bent by placing it against a solid surface. It's easiest to place the bend line at the surface's edge to obtain a sharp bend directly at the marked line.


Install the brackets at their correct positions, position the armrest over the brackets and mark the portion of the bracket that protrudes beyond the edge of the armrest.


The brackets can be cut a number of ways, but lacking more appropriate tooling, carefully sneak kitchen shears to the work area and cut the bracket a full 1/8" inside the marked line. The stainless steel (or raw brass) strip is only .030" thick and actually cuts easily.


Cutting the excess material will distort the bracket edge. Tap it against a hard surface to uniformly flatten the edge.


Sand the top side with 220 grit sandpaper to prepare it for bonding.


Sand the rear of the armrest with 220 grit sandpaper where the brackets will be positioned.


Wipe down the bracket and armrest surfaces to prepare them for bonding. Lacquer thinner is used here, but naptha, pure alcohol, or even dish soap and warm water can be used. Do not use rubbing alcohol as it contains oil that will be left behind when the alcohol evaporates.


The brackets are re-installed once more. Notice the numbers are still present on the bottom side of the brackets.


A small quantity of JB Weld is dispensed in equal amounts on a disposable surface and mixed with a small screwdriver tip.


Small pieces of thicker cardboard can be cut and fitted under the bracket tips to prevent them from being pushed toward the head when the armrest is weighted down to secure it while the JB Weld solidifies. This depends on how tightly the hooks secure the brackets. They don't have to fit tightly, as the armrest will work fine even if the clips are loose at this point. Apply the JB Weld to the top of the bracket so it is about 1/16" thick; a cleaner installation will be achieved if you stay back about 1/8" from the bend.


Three small strips of tape can be applied to hold the armrest at the correct location over the brackets and to prevent it from shifting when pressure is applied to the armrest top surface.


A bit of padding (folded paper towel is used here) is placed between the bracket locations to protect the armrest finish and distribute pressure before adding a weight over the armrest. The weight will distribute the JB Weld between the brackets and the armrest surface. Leave it undisturbed for 24 hours.


After 24 hours the armrest is removed and the excess adhesive can be cut away, sanded, and smoothed with the sandpaper and 0000 steel wool. The steel wool can also be used to polish the visible edges and top surface of the armrest.
