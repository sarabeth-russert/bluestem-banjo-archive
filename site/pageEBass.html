Conventional Electric Bass

I enjoy the occasional foray into playing the electric bass and occasionally get a call for a gig requiring electric bass, so I've always kept an electric bass in the stable. After playing an inexpensive import for a few years I decided to build my own based on my style and liking. The result was a walnut bodied fretted bass that I enjoyed playing for several years. In 2012 I decided I would revisit that instrument and address a few changes that I wanted to make to make it more to my current preferances. The original version had a body with a short upper horn, so the main points would be a new body with a longer top horn to eliminate a bit of "neck dive" and conversion to fretless. I don't play often enough to feel comfortable with a fingerboard with no visual referance, so the new version has a "lined" or "flush fret" fingerboard. A few other modifications included front mounting of the pickup to eliminate rear mounting screws, mounting volume control and output jack in a custom front cover to eliminate the need for a rear control cavity cover, and a slight redesign of the brass string mounting bar to eliminate excess thickness. I will switch out the pickup mounting screws with flat head screws to eliminate the protruding heads, but they really don't influence my playing comfort. All in all, a vast improvement on an already great sounding bass, so mission accomplished!

Bluestem Electric Bass features:

  • 34" scale length with ebony constant radius flush fret fingerboard inlayed with .023" maple strips
  • Walnut neck reenforced with a single 1/4" thick 2024 T4 aircraft aluminum bar tapered from 1/2" to 5/8"
  • Two piece walnut contoured body
  • GHS Pressurewound medium gauge strings
  • Schaller deluxe enclosed tuning machines
  • Ebony headstock overlay with Mother of pearl Celtic Dog inlay
  • 1/8" diameter inlaid position markers in side of neck
  • Recessed Dunlop Straploc strap buttons
  • Hand shaped raw brass patina finished adjustable height bridge and string retainer
  • Duncan quarter pounder pickup encased in walnut block serves as a string ramp
  • String retainer and components grounded through hidden drilled passages
  • Single CTS volume control with custom walnut control knob
  • Switchcraft 1/4" output jack
