Woodworking for the "Maker Epoch", or whatever other label you'd like to see it wear...

Periodically the "cultural spokesmen" of the day suddenly look beyond themselves and see something bright and shiney that they were previously unaware of. They gently urge us to reach the same conclusions about this "new" phenomenon, but in reality it's been here all along. For eons of time there have always been those who engage themselves in the pursuit of handcraft; the reasons miriad, from economic and practical to the artistic and creative. The bottom line is that some are simply called to it.

It is in that spirit that I participate in woodworking, and I find it enormously gratifying to live in the moment among things created by my own hand, be it workshop tools, our home filled with one-off furniture designs, or creating music on instruments of my own making.

I particularly enjoy the process of finding my own equilibrium point between simplicity of design and artful embellishment. That aspect finds it's way into all of my endevours, and I hope you also choose to embrace those ideals.

Workbench 2016

  • Economical to build, using select and better construction grade 2 by 4s and 2 by 6s
  • Dowel keyed bolt together design utilizing 3/8" all thread rod is ultra-simple, but rock solid
  • 64" by 24" by 35" tall shown, but adapt it to suit YOUR needs!
  • Total weight of the bench as shown is a respectable 175 pounds
  • Top is a solid 3-1/8" thick, full row of 3/4" dog holes shown
  • Front surface totally flush for use with leg vise or other clamping means
  • No vise is shown, but bench will accomodate whatever you like with a bit of thought and planning
  • Lower 1 by 3 rod channel covers serve as built-in lower shelf support
